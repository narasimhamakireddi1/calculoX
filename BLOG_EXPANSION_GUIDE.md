```
"What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple numerical index calculated from your height and weight using a standardized formula. While BMI is not a perfect measure of overall health, it is a widely-used screening tool by doctors and health organizations globally to identify potential weight-related health risks.

The formula is straightforward: BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height² (m²).

For example, a person weighing 70 kg with height 1.75m would have: 
BMI = 70 ÷ (1.75 × 1.75) = 70 ÷ 3.06 = 22.88

This calculation is the same worldwide and provides a quick assessment of whether someone is underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese based on standardized categories."
(~150 words)
```

```
Q: "What is a healthy BMI for Indians?"
A: "For Indian adults, a healthy BMI according to Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) guidelines is 18.5-22.9. This differs from Western BMI cutoffs (18.5-24.9) because South Asians, including Indians, have higher body fat percentages at lower BMI values compared to Western populations.

BMI 23-27.4 is considered overweight in Indian standards, and BMI 27.5+ is classified as obese. These stricter cutoffs exist because Indians face higher risks of diabetes and heart disease at lower BMI levels compared to Caucasian populations.

For example, an Indian woman at BMI 25 might have the same health risk profile as a Caucasian woman at BMI 27-28. This is why your doctor may recommend weight loss at BMI 23-24 for Indians, whereas Western standards would say you're still 'normal'.

Beyond BMI, also monitor: waist circumference (should be <90 cm for men, <80 cm for women in India), overall fitness level, and metabolic markers like fasting blood sugar and cholesterol."
```
